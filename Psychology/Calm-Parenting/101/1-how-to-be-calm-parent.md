### Calm Parent:

Calm does not mean not lively or hilarious. Calm parent aims to be more peaceful and less reactive inside. To be a better role model, help them build a brain and nervous system that can self-regulate.

### Practices:
1. **Regulate your own emotions.**  
    -  When child has strong emotions and even if he misbehaves, you should not give in to the temptation to throw tantrums. 
    - Parents are the role model, so if you can regulate your emotions, your child will learn your way. They will still feel jealous, but will manage in healthy way.
         
2. **Stay warmly connected to child.**
   - when you connect deeply with child, child wants to protect relationship and cooperates. 
   - They need to *choose* to do, instead of us *make* them do. That avoids Endless series of bribes, threats and Power Struggle.
3. **Coach instead of controlling.**

    - A coach teaches, supports to be best, does not punish. Creates opportunities to grow and celebrate.
    - Controlling = Threatening Punishment. Child does not own/choose the behavior and delays learning empathy.

    *Calm parents are not perfect humans. But the commitment to self regulation, connection and coaching changes behavior, one action at a time.*

### The parenting skills that help you become Calm Parent:
1. **Returning Yourself to Calm**
2. **Emotion Coaching**

#### Returning Yourself to Calm
*The Low Road* - Stressed, exhausted, resentful. When you are wringing out an apology out of children. Fight-or-flight mode, kids look like enemy. Fuse is so short. Feels justified to have your own little tantrum

*The High Road* - Feeling good, emotionally generous.Responding squabbles with patience, understanding and sense of humour. Enjoys being a parent.

Step 1 - Train yourself to notice when you start to slide *The Low Road*.

Step 2 - Not to take any action until you've recentered yourself. 

This is hard. But start small with some easy techniques:

*Take Five* - Count Five small deep breaths. Imaging breathing out tension. Conscious breathing really helps.
Do this when your children are fighting, before you intervene.

Kids Emotions high &uarr; -> They think emergency -> Parents think emergency -> X

Eg: Son Pushes toddler -> Parents (fight-or-flight) -> son looks like foe -> rescue baby -> son flees and parent pursues, threatens.

The more it is repeated. Kids think life is an emergency. They build a brain geared for self-protection -> more aggressive.

When we **yell**, we model that behavior. Children learn to yell at each other. They respond to conflicts by yelling, blaming. Regulating your emotions is the hardest thing and it is always a work-in-progress. 

    When you react calmly, They learn that the situation seems like an emergency but is not and they will listen to their siblings because they know they will be listened to

#### Emotion Coaching

It is not enough to teach just self-regulation. Kids also need help with tough emotions like jealousy, fear, anger - to feel safe to feel their emotions.

    Once we let ourselves feel an emotion, it begins to dissipate. If we push it away, stuffing it unconsciously, we are no longer in control -> we get triggered and explode.

Emotion Coaching helps kids learn to calm themselves and understand sibling point of view and put their needs into words instead of lashing out.

Emotional intelligence skills begin with child's ability to soothe themselves when upset. Babies brain build the neural pathways to soothe their upsets, everytime you soothe them.

- If he is having a hard time, take him in your arms and breath deeply and make a soothing noise. As he regains composure, suggest him to breathe with you.

**Myth** - Babies learn themselves to self-soothe. - Brain Research **Debunked**

***Empathize with child's emotions*** - this is the most important skill. It helps soothe child and develop empathy.

Virtually all children will be able to understand others emotions through mirror neurons, but they don't learn to feel safe with emotions -> Other peoples upset feelings scare them.
    
    Empathy -> he feels close -> accept limits and cooperates. -> feels safe with emotions -> self regulates.

    Contrast. child feels his feelings are not okay -> stuffs them -> no conscious control -> bad behavior

**How to Empathize**:

- Recognize child feeling with your heart not just with words. **Suspend your Own Agenda**. Truly listen. 

        We often see others feelings as overreacting and making our lives difficult. It is not because we are unkind. We just see them through the lens of our needs & desires

Acknowledge her perspective with understanding - even if you don't agree. 

*~ You may not be 100% empathic all the time. Like all goals try to improve. ~*

**Myth** - Accepting emotions creates *Drama Queens*. Its the opposite **Debunked**

        When Parents open their hearts to allow child to express emotions
        -> child learns its okay to feel emotions and they are normal, not dangerous.
        -> child can regulate behavior because emotions are under conscious control, not stuffed up.
        -> uses words to express feelings. He might yell but that is huge step from physically lashing out.
        -> Learns Anger Management.

*Not suggesting to allow any behavior in home. Just that all emotions can be accepted. So that child can make friends with emotions.*
